## Target Audience

**Demographics:**
- Women 30–55, primarily USA
- Middle-class, $50K-$120K household income
- Moms, professionals, women entering midlife body changes
- Sizes 6–18+ (straight to plus-size)

**Two Core Personas:**

### Maria (42) — "The Hiding Mom"
- 3 kids, body changed significantly after pregnancies
- Hasn't worn a bikini in years — sticks to one-pieces and coverups
- Dreads pool parties and beach vacations
- Deep insecurity about lower belly ("FUPA", "apron belly", "mommy pouch")
- Secretly wants to feel feminine and sexy again
- Biggest fear: being judged by other moms or her husband noticing her belly

### Jennifer (35) — "The Defeated Shopper"
- Tried dozens of swimsuits, returned most
- Knows exactly what she wants but can't find it
- Frustrated that "flattering" swimwear means "ugly" or "grandma style"
- Wants modern, cute design that actually works for her body
- Biggest fear: ordering online and it looking nothing like the photos

## Copy Rules

1. **Name the pain boldly** — FUPA, apron belly, mommy pouch. She Googles these terms. Don't be polite about it.
2. **Show empathy, not pity** — "We know the struggle" not "Poor you"
3. **Use her exact language** — from Reddit: "hiding behind coverups", "haven't worn a bikini in years"
4. **Social proof from real bodies** — not size-2 models. She needs to see HER body in it.
5. **Don't oversell** — she's been burned. Be honest about what it does and doesn't do.
6. **Focus on the FEELING** — confidence, freedom, femininity. Not just features.
7. **Beach/pool moments** — paint the scene: playing with kids, walking to the pool, partner's reaction.

## Visual Direction (for Higgsfield / Ad Creatives)

**DO:**
- Woman 35–50, natural body (soft belly, curves, real proportions)
- Beach, pool, or tropical vacation setting
- Warm golden sunlight, vacation vibes
- Genuine smile, candid moment — playing with kids, laughing, walking on sand
- Show the high-waist fit clearly — how it sits on the tummy
- Confidence radiating — she's not hiding, she's enjoying herself
- Water/pool edge shots — she's IN the scene, not posing for a catalog

**DON'T:**
- No fitness models or flat stomachs
- No overly posed/sexy shots — this is about confidence, not seduction
- No studio/white background — this needs real-life warmth
- No coverups or sarongs — show her actually wearing just the bikini
- No text overlay unless requested
